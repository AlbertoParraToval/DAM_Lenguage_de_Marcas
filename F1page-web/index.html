What is the aim with these new regulations?

The 2022 regulations are - or that is the goal - the salvation of Formula 1. It is the first technical regulation created by Liberty Media, which came to power in 2017 after ousting Bernie Ecclestone, and among its creators have been Pat Symonds, Nikolas Tombazis or Ross Brawn, former heads of teams like Renault, Ferrari, BrawnGP... With this new regulation, the aim is to improve Formula 1, starting with something that fails: the design of the cars themselves.

The lack of on-track action we have seen in recent years (2021 will be just the exception to the rule, although there will still be little overtaking) is blamed, among other things, on the single-seaters. Current F1 cars, when followed by another car, generate turbulence that can cause a loss of up to 50% of downforce in the F1 car trying to overtake. As a result, the overtake often does not take place or the driver is forced to lift his foot to avoid excessive damage to his tyres.

The new 2022 F1 cars, thanks to all the changes made after two years of work in the wind tunnel and with computational fluid dynamics (CFD), will reduce this turbulence to 10%. If achieved, overtaking would be much less artificial - although DRS remains - and there would be more wheel-to-wheel fighting and, ultimately, more action.

What are these changes to the regulation?

2021

2022

1.Front part
Nose of the horn becomes much wider and with a more pronounced droop.
Redesigned endplates.
Redesigned suspension arms.
Front wheel deflectors added.
Modified brake air intakes.
2.Lateral Part
18-inch tyres.
Return of the cups, except that they must rotate together with the wheel.
Elimination of flat floor.
Start of floor at a considerable height.
3.Rear part
Larger rear spoiler with curved lines
Despite what has been seen, DRS remains in place
New diffuser
4.View from above
The size of the single-seaters remains the same.
The single-seaters are now about 60 kg heavier than the current ones.
From above we can clearly see that there are no side deflectors.
